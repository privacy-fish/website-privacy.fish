privacy.fish is designed around local control and standard secure transport tools, not around a universal consumer UX.

## Supported environments

The service is intended to work with:

- Linux
- macOS
- Windows
- Android

iPhone is **not** supported.

## Why iPhone is excluded

The platform depends on client-side tooling and SSH / SFTP-oriented workflows. On iPhone there is no practical way to support the intended client model without collapsing back into a more provider-controlled experience.

Rather than pretending to offer feature parity everywhere, privacy.fish is explicit about the limitation.

## What the workflow feels like

Compared with a typical hosted mailbox:

- there is no webmail inbox,
- you download mail instead of browsing it remotely,
- optional tooling can bridge downloaded mail into familiar desktop mail clients,
- your own devices become the primary place where mail lives long term.

## Who should feel comfortable here

The best fit is someone who values privacy more than smooth onboarding and who already thinks in terms of files, keys, and local control.

If your priority is frictionless access from any browser on any device, this is the wrong product.
